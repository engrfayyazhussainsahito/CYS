# Week 16 Overview — Capstone

## Purpose
Week 16 converts the course into an integrated, defensible capstone portfolio. It assesses whether learners can connect technical evidence, risk reasoning, governance, incident response, privacy/legal escalation, and executive communication.

## Research Basis
- NIST CSF 2.0 for Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover structure.
- NIST SP 800-37 Rev. 2 for RMF lifecycle and continuous monitoring.
- NIST SP 800-53A Rev. 5 for control assessment and evidence quality.
- NIST SP 800-55 Vol. 1 for selecting security measures.
- NIST SP 800-115 for planning/conducting/reporting technical security assessment.
- NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 3 for incident response aligned to CSF 2.0.
- CISA CPGs for practical baseline expectations.
- NIST SP 800-181 Rev. 1 NICE Framework for cybersecurity work/skills language.

## Planned Lessons
1. Lesson 16.1 — Capstone Scenario, Charter, and Success Criteria
2. Lesson 16.2 — Enterprise Security Assessment and Risk Register
3. Lesson 16.3 — Detection, Incident Response, and Forensic Evidence Drill
4. Lesson 16.4 — Governance, Privacy, Legal, and Third-Party Executive Brief
5. Lesson 16.5 — Final Portfolio, Defense, and Career Roadmap

## Deliverables
- Project charter
- System context package
- Risk register
- Evidence notebook
- Detection and incident response packet
- Governance brief
- Final portfolio and defense artifacts
