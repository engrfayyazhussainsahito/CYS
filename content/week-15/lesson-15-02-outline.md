# Lesson 15.2 — Post-Quantum Cryptography and Crypto-Agility

## Purpose
Teach post-quantum migration as a defensive engineering, governance, inventory, and risk-prioritization problem rather than as a simple algorithm swap.

## Research baseline
- NIST finalized FIPS 203, FIPS 204, and FIPS 205 in August 2024 for ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA.
- NIST/NCCoE migration guidance emphasizes cryptographic discovery, inventory, risk prioritization, interoperability testing, and non-production validation.
- NIST IR 8547 provides a draft transition approach from quantum-vulnerable standards to post-quantum standards.
- NSA CNSA Suite 2.0 reinforces that organizations with long-lived or national-security-relevant cryptography must plan, prepare, and budget early.
