# Week 15 Overview — Advanced and Emerging Topics

## Purpose
Teach advanced/emerging cybersecurity topics as disciplined risk evaluation, not hype reaction or exploit instruction.

## Research basis
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework 1.0 — AI risk governance, mapping, measurement, and management.
- NIST AI 600-1 — Generative AI Profile for AI RMF, including information security, privacy, information integrity, and value-chain/component risks.
- OWASP Top 10 for Large Language Model Applications — LLM application-security vocabulary and risk categories.
- NIST FIPS 203/204/205 announcement — first finalized post-quantum cryptography standards.
- NIST SP 1800-38 — post-quantum migration and cryptographic discovery planning.
- NIST SP 800-207 and CISA Zero Trust Maturity Model 2.0 — zero trust architecture and maturity planning.
- NIST SP 800-82 Rev. 3 — operational technology security.
- NIST IR 8425 — IoT core baseline capabilities.
- CISA Secure by Design — manufacturer accountability, transparency, and customer security outcomes.
- NIST SP 800-160 Vol. 2 Rev. 1 — cyber-resilient systems engineering.

## Lesson map
1. Lesson 15.1 — AI Security, LLM Risk, and Model Governance.
2. Lesson 15.2 — Post-Quantum Cryptography and Crypto-Agility.
3. Lesson 15.3 — Zero Trust at Scale, SASE, and Identity-Centric Architecture.
4. Lesson 15.4 — OT, IoT, and Cyber-Physical Security.
5. Lesson 15.5 — Secure by Design, Cyber Resilience, and Future-Ready Programs.

## Boundary
No exploit walkthroughs, unauthorized testing, offensive automation, model-abuse instructions, unsafe scanning, or real-target procedures.

## Deliverables
- Emerging-risk evaluation worksheet.
- AI/LLM application risk map.
- PQC readiness inventory.
- Zero-trust maturity snapshot.
- OT/IoT safety and availability worksheet.
- Secure-by-design checklist.
- Cyber-resilience roadmap.
