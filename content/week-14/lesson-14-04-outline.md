# Lesson 14.4 — Cyber Law, Regulation, and Incident Reporting

## Purpose
Teach cybersecurity learners how legal, regulatory, contractual, privacy, securities, critical-infrastructure, financial-sector, and product-security reporting duties affect incident-response decisions without providing legal advice.

## Research anchors
- NIST CSF 2.0 — governance, legal/regulatory requirements, risk communication.
- NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 3 — current incident-response framing.
- GDPR Articles 33/34 — personal-data breach notification and communication concepts.
- SEC cybersecurity incident disclosure guidance and Item 1.05 materiality framing.
- CIRCIA rulemaking materials — critical-infrastructure reporting readiness and harmonization.
- NIS2 Article 23 — significant-incident staged reporting.
- DORA — ICT risk and major ICT-related incident reporting for financial entities.
- FTC Safeguards Rule notification requirement.
- EU Cyber Resilience Act and ENISA Single Reporting Platform materials.

## Lab
A safe local JSON/Python lab that reviews fictional incidents and generates possible external-reporting workstreams and escalation prompts. It performs no network activity, no scanning, no legal analysis, and no real incident assessment.
