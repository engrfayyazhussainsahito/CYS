# Week 14 Overview — Governance, Risk, Privacy, Law

## Purpose
Teach cybersecurity governance as the decision, accountability, evidence, and reporting system around security and privacy risk.

## Research basis
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 — Govern function and common cybersecurity risk language.
- NIST SP 800-37 Rev. 2 — Risk Management Framework and continuous monitoring.
- NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 — security and privacy control catalog.
- NIST Privacy Framework — privacy risk management outcomes.
- CISA Cybersecurity Performance Goals 2.0 — prioritized baseline cybersecurity practices.
- European Commission GDPR/data protection guidance — personal-data accountability concepts.
- EU NIS2 Directive guidance — cybersecurity governance, risk management, and incident reporting in critical sectors.
- CISA CIRCIA materials — evolving U.S. critical-infrastructure incident-reporting requirements.
- SEC cybersecurity disclosure rules — governance and material-incident disclosure context for covered public companies.
- DORA and CRA official EU materials — resilience, reporting, third-party, and product-security regulation examples.

## Lesson map
1. Lesson 14.1 — Cybersecurity Governance from First Principles.
2. Lesson 14.2 — Risk Management, Controls, and Assurance.
3. Lesson 14.3 — Privacy Engineering and Data Protection.
4. Lesson 14.4 — Cyber Law, Regulation, and Incident Reporting.
5. Lesson 14.5 — Third-Party Risk, Audit Evidence, and Governance Reporting.

## Boundary
Educational governance and risk-management content only. Not legal advice. Escalate jurisdiction-specific obligations to counsel/compliance/privacy owners.

## Deliverables
- Governance RACI.
- Risk-register entries.
- Control and evidence map.
- Privacy data-flow worksheet.
- Regulatory/contractual reporting escalation matrix.
- Third-party risk review checklist.
- Executive risk summary.
