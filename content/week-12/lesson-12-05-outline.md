# Lesson 12.5 — Reporting, Lessons Learned, and Tabletop Exercises

## Purpose
Teach students how to convert incident evidence into defensible reports, after-action reviews, corrective actions, and tabletop exercises that improve incident-response capability.

## Research anchors
- NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 3 — Incident response as CSF 2.0-aligned cybersecurity risk management.
- NIST CSF 2.0 — Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover outcomes.
- CISA Cybersecurity Incident and Vulnerability Response Playbooks — standardized identify/coordinate/remediate/recover/track procedures.
- NIST SP 800-84 — design, development, conduct, and evaluation of TT&E events.
- NIST SP 800-184 — recovery planning, playbooks, testing, improvement, resilience.
- CISA Tabletop Exercise Packages — scenario/module-question structures for response and recovery discussions.
- CISA incident reporting guidance and CIRCIA resources — current reporting-preparation context.

## Major concepts
1. Reporting as a chain of reasoning.
2. Fact/inference/uncertainty separation.
3. Audience-specific reporting.
4. Defensible timeline construction.
5. Evidence-to-claim mapping.
6. Incident closure criteria.
7. Corrective-action registers.
8. Lessons learned without scapegoating.
9. Tabletop objectives, roles, injects, and evaluation criteria.
10. After-action reports as improvement tools.

## Lab
A safe fictional local Python lab builds an after-action package, validates unsupported claims, creates an executive summary, groups corrective actions, and produces tabletop injects.
