Week 16 · Lesson 16.3

Detection, Incident Response, and Forensic Evidence Drill

The capstone drill turns scattered fictional telemetry into a disciplined incident-analysis package: alert triage, evidence preservation, timeline reconstruction, confidence statements, containment recommendations, recovery checks, and stakeholder communication boundaries.

Estimated reading time95–130 minutes
Difficulty levelMS capstone integration
Required backgroundWeeks 11–12 and Lessons 16.1–16.2 recommended
Lab modeSafe local fictional evidence and triage drill
Boundary: This lesson uses only fictional, course-provided records. It does not authorize live scanning, endpoint collection, memory capture, account access, cloud-console access, malware execution, third-party contact, notification decisions, or investigation of real users.

1. Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Convert fictional alerts into a triage queue with priority, confidence, evidence references, and open questions.
  • Separate detection, investigation, forensic preservation, incident response, recovery, and reporting activities.
  • Build an incident timeline from multiple evidence sources without overstating what the evidence proves.
  • Identify when privacy, legal, compliance, communications, and business owners must be consulted.
  • Recommend containment and recovery actions that preserve evidence and explain business impact.
  • Produce a capstone-ready incident packet with facts, inferences, assumptions, unknowns, and next decisions.

2. Research Basis

This lesson is grounded in current defensive guidance. NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 3 frames incident response as part of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 risk-management cycle and emphasizes preparation, detection, response, recovery, reporting, notification, and communications. NIST SP 800-86 describes the forensic process as collection, examination, analysis, and reporting from an IT incident-response perspective. NIST SP 800-92 explains why sound log management matters for enterprise security operations. CISA's federal incident and vulnerability response playbooks provide standardized response procedures to identify, coordinate, remediate, recover, and track mitigations. MITRE ATT&CK gives a common vocabulary for adversary tactics, techniques, detections, and data sources.

Research implication: A strong incident drill does not ask learners to prove a dramatic breach. It asks them to preserve evidence, state what is known, identify uncertainty, control harm, coordinate decisions, and document why each action follows from the available evidence.

3. Drill Model

Lesson 16.3 combines four capstone skills:

  1. Detection: understand what each alert indicates and what data source produced it.
  2. Investigation: correlate alerts, test alternative explanations, and decide whether incident declaration is justified.
  3. Forensic evidence handling: preserve relevant records, document integrity, and maintain chain of custody for the fictional drill.
  4. Response planning: recommend containment, recovery, and communication steps with owners and constraints.

The exercise is intentionally evidence-driven. You are not graded for assuming the worst. You are graded for building a defensible case packet.

4. Capstone Detection-to-Response Workflow

flowchart TD
  A[Fictional telemetry package] --> B[Alert triage]
  B --> C[Evidence manifest review]
  C --> D[Timeline reconstruction]
  D --> E[Hypothesis and alternatives]
  E --> F[Incident declaration decision]
  F --> G[Containment recommendations]
  G --> H[Recovery readiness checks]
  H --> I[Fact/inference/unknown incident packet]
  I --> J[Governance and executive handoff]

The order is deliberate. Jumping to containment before evidence review can destroy useful context. Waiting too long to contain a confirmed active compromise can increase harm. The analyst's job is to make the trade-off explicit.

5. Roles and Decision Rights

A detection analyst does not own every decision. In the capstone, assign decisions to accountable roles:

RolePrimary decisionsShould not do alone
SOC analystInitial triage, evidence references, timeline draft, escalation recommendationDeclare data breach, notify customers, change production systems
Incident commander or IR leadIncident declaration, coordination, response tempo, situation reportsMake legal/privacy conclusions without counsel/privacy input
System or application ownerContainment impact, service operations, recovery validationDestroy evidence before preservation review
Privacy/legal/complianceNotification analysis, privilege, regulatory reporting, sensitive-data handlingAssume technical facts not supported by evidence
Executive sponsorBusiness risk acceptance, outage trade-offs, resource prioritizationOverride safety/evidence constraints without documented risk decision

6. Alert Triage

Alert triage asks: What does this alert prove, how reliable is it, what asset is affected, what evidence supports it, what alternative explanations exist, and what decision is needed? A good triage record includes:

  • Alert ID, time, data source, affected asset, and owner.
  • Severity and confidence as separate fields.
  • Relevant evidence references, not screenshots alone.
  • Potential ATT&CK tactic/technique language if it helps communication.
  • Business impact path and privacy/legal escalation flags.
  • Immediate questions that must be answered before stronger claims are made.
Common failure: Severity is not certainty. A critical alert with weak evidence may deserve fast review, but the report must still say what is unknown.

7. Source Context and Correlation

Correlated evidence is stronger than isolated evidence, but correlation still requires judgment. In this drill:

  • Identity logs can support claims about sign-ins, MFA state, impossible travel patterns, and account usage.
  • Endpoint alerts can support claims about suspicious process behavior on scoped hosts.
  • Cloud audit logs can support claims about administrative actions, exports, and configuration changes.
  • Network logs can support claims about VPN, firewall, or connection patterns.
  • Helpdesk notes can support user-reported symptoms but need corroboration.
  • Backup logs can support recovery-readiness claims but do not prove incident root cause.

One evidence type rarely answers the whole incident. Strong analysis explains how sources complement or contradict each other.

8. Forensic Evidence Handling

Forensic evidence handling in this course is about preservation and traceability, not collecting from live systems. The capstone evidence manifest should include:

9. Timeline Reconstruction

A timeline is not just a sorted list. It should distinguish event occurrence from evidence collection. If an alert occurred at 08:11 and the export was collected at 09:05, both times matter. Your timeline should include:

  • Event time in UTC.
  • Evidence collection time in UTC.
  • Source system and confidence.
  • Actor/account if supported by records.
  • Asset or service affected.
  • Alternate explanation or missing context.

Do not hide conflicting timestamps. Conflicts are useful because they identify clock-sync, ingestion, retention, or interpretation problems.

10. Hypotheses and Alternative Explanations

The capstone incident packet should include a primary hypothesis and alternatives. Example:

Primary hypothesis: A privileged account was misused to access the patient portal environment, execute unusual administrative activity, and create a bulk export job. Alternatives: scheduled maintenance was undocumented; the helpdesk report is unrelated; the export was legitimate but poorly logged; the endpoint alert is a false positive caused by an approved script.

Professional investigation does not prove the hypothesis by ignoring alternatives. It states what additional evidence would raise or lower confidence.

11. ATT&CK as Vocabulary, Not Proof

MITRE ATT&CK can help describe adversary behavior, but mapping an alert to a tactic does not prove an adversary was present. Use ATT&CK labels to organize thinking:

  • What behavior category does the evidence resemble?
  • Which data source would strengthen or weaken the hypothesis?
  • Which detection or mitigation gap does the behavior expose?
  • How should this feed future detection engineering or purple-team handoff?

Bad use: “This is ATT&CK, therefore breach.” Better use: “The behavior resembles valid-account misuse and collection. Identity, cloud audit, endpoint, and helpdesk evidence should be correlated before claiming compromise or data exposure.”

12. Incident Declaration Decision

Incident declaration is a management decision supported by evidence. Consider declaration when:

  • Multiple high-confidence sources point to unauthorized activity.
  • A critical asset or sensitive data path may be affected.
  • Containment requires coordination across teams.
  • Evidence retention or legal/privacy obligations may be time-sensitive.
  • Recovery or business-continuity decisions may be needed.

For the capstone, declare an incident for analysis when the evidence pattern justifies coordinated response. Do not declare a breach, regulatory event, or customer-notification requirement unless the fictional facts support that and the lesson explicitly asks for it.

13. Containment Without Evidence Destruction

Containment recommendations should be least-destructive, authorized, and reversible where possible. Examples include:

  • Ask the identity owner to revoke active sessions and review privileged-account state.
  • Preserve identity, endpoint, cloud, network, helpdesk, and backup records before expiration.
  • Isolate only if the asset owner and IR lead agree the benefit outweighs business disruption.
  • Freeze risky scheduled jobs pending business-owner review.
  • Document the expected impact of each action on operations and evidence.

Do not recommend wiping or rebuilding systems before evidence preservation and recovery planning are addressed.

14. Recovery and Validation

Recovery is not “the alert stopped.” Recovery requires validation that affected services are safe enough to operate and that control gaps have been addressed or consciously accepted. Recovery evidence can include:

  • Known-good configuration or backup status.
  • Credential/session reset confirmation.
  • Audit-log retention confirmation.
  • Detection coverage after remediation.
  • Business owner validation that the service works.
  • Residual-risk note for unresolved issues.

Backup success is not restore success. A backup job log supports readiness, but a restore test or recovery exercise supports confidence.

15. Situation Reports and Escalation

A capstone situation report should be short and disciplined:

  • Facts: what records directly show.
  • Inferences: reasonable conclusions from correlated records.
  • Unknowns: missing facts that affect decisions.
  • Actions taken/recommended: evidence preservation, containment, recovery, and owner actions.
  • Decisions needed: declare, contain, preserve, recover, escalate, notify, or accept risk.

Never write “data was exfiltrated” when the evidence only shows a suspicious export job. State the narrower fact and the question that remains.

16. Evidence Quality Review

Before final submission, review each claim with this checklist:

  1. Can a reviewer trace the claim to an evidence ID?
  2. Is the evidence source named and scoped?
  3. Are timestamps UTC and sorted correctly?
  4. Is chain of custody documented for preserved artifacts?
  5. Does the claim separate fact from inference?
  6. Does the recommendation name the decision owner?
  7. Does the response action preserve or intentionally trade off evidence?
  8. Are privacy/legal/compliance flags handled as escalation questions, not legal conclusions?

17. Lesson 16.3 Deliverables

By the end of this lesson, your capstone package should contain:

  • Alert triage table with priority, confidence, owner, and evidence references.
  • Evidence manifest with integrity and chain-of-custody fields.
  • Incident timeline separating event time from collection time.
  • Primary hypothesis and at least two alternative explanations.
  • Incident declaration recommendation with rationale.
  • Containment and recovery recommendation list with owners and business impact notes.
  • Situation report separating facts, inferences, assumptions, and unknowns.

Step 2 — Create detection_ir_evidence_drill.py

#!/usr/bin/env python3
"""
Safe local capstone drill for Lesson 16.3.
Uses fictional JSON only. Performs no network activity, scanning,
authentication, exploitation, cloud access, or real evidence collection.
"""
import json
from pathlib import Path
from datetime import datetime, timezone

PACKAGE = Path("detection_ir_evidence_package.json")
OUTPUT = Path("detection_ir_drill_output.json")

SEVERITY = {"low": 1, "medium": 2, "high": 3, "critical": 4}
CONFIDENCE = {"low": 0, "medium": 1, "high": 2}
CRITICALITY = {"low": 0, "medium": 1, "high": 2, "critical": 3}
REQUIRED_EVIDENCE_FIELDS = ["id", "type", "source", "collected_at_utc", "collector", "hash_recorded", "chain_of_custody", "retention_days"]
PROHIBITED_KEYWORDS = ["live scanning", "credential use", "network requests", "malware execution", "cloud console access", "vendor contact", "real user investigation"]


def parse_time(value):
    if not isinstance(value, str) or not value.endswith("Z"):
        raise ValueError(f"Timestamp must be UTC and end with Z: {value}")
    return datetime.fromisoformat(value.replace("Z", "+00:00")).astimezone(timezone.utc)


def load_package():
    if not PACKAGE.exists():
        raise SystemExit(f"Missing {PACKAGE}. Create the fictional JSON file first.")
    return json.loads(PACKAGE.read_text(encoding="utf-8"))


def index_by_id(items):
    return {item["id"]: item for item in items}


def check_scope(data):
    warnings = []
    boundary = data.get("scope_boundary", "").lower()
    if "fictional" not in boundary or "no live" not in boundary:
        warnings.append("Scope boundary should explicitly state fictional-only and no-live-activity limits.")
    for method in data.get("allowed_methods", []):
        lowered = method.lower()
        for keyword in PROHIBITED_KEYWORDS:
            if keyword in lowered:
                warnings.append(f"Allowed method appears unsafe: {method}")
    return warnings


def evidence_warnings(evidence):
    warnings = []
    ids = set()
    for item in evidence:
        eid = item.get("id", "unknown")
        if eid in ids:
            warnings.append(f"Duplicate evidence ID: {eid}")
        ids.add(eid)
        for field in REQUIRED_EVIDENCE_FIELDS:
            if field not in item or item[field] in (None, "", []):
                warnings.append(f"{eid}: missing evidence field {field}")
        try:
            parse_time(item.get("collected_at_utc"))
        except Exception as exc:
            warnings.append(f"{eid}: invalid collected_at_utc: {exc}")
        if not item.get("hash_recorded"):
            warnings.append(f"{eid}: hash/integrity record missing")
        if len(item.get("chain_of_custody", [])) < 2:
            warnings.append(f"{eid}: chain of custody has fewer than two entries")
        if item.get("retention_days", 0) < 30:
            warnings.append(f"{eid}: retention window is short; preserve/export promptly")
    return warnings


def score_alert(alert, assets, evidence_index):
    asset = assets.get(alert.get("asset_id"), {})
    score = SEVERITY.get(alert.get("severity"), 0) * 2
    score += CONFIDENCE.get(alert.get("confidence"), 0)
    score += CRITICALITY.get(asset.get("criticality"), 0)
    if len(alert.get("evidence_refs", [])) >= 2:
        score += 1
    if alert.get("requires_privacy_review"):
        score += 1
    if any("Exfiltration" in x or "Collection" in x for x in alert.get("attack_mapping", [])):
        score += 1
    unresolved_refs = [ref for ref in alert.get("evidence_refs", []) if ref not in evidence_index]
    return score, unresolved_refs


def band(score):
    if score >= 12:
        return "priority-1"
    if score >= 9:
        return "priority-2"
    if score >= 6:
        return "priority-3"
    return "watch"


def build_triage(alerts, assets, evidence_index):
    records = []
    warnings = []
    for alert in alerts:
        score, unresolved = score_alert(alert, assets, evidence_index)
        if unresolved:
            warnings.append(f"{alert.get('id')}: unresolved evidence refs: {', '.join(unresolved)}")
        asset = assets.get(alert.get("asset_id"), {})
        records.append({
            "alert_id": alert.get("id"),
            "timestamp_utc": alert.get("timestamp_utc"),
            "source": alert.get("source"),
            "asset": asset.get("name", alert.get("asset_id")),
            "owner": asset.get("owner", "unknown"),
            "summary": alert.get("summary"),
            "attack_mapping": alert.get("attack_mapping", []),
            "score": score,
            "priority": band(score),
            "privacy_review": bool(alert.get("requires_privacy_review")),
            "evidence_refs": alert.get("evidence_refs", []),
            "initial_questions": build_questions(alert, asset)
        })
    return sorted(records, key=lambda r: (r["score"], r["timestamp_utc"]), reverse=True), warnings


def build_questions(alert, asset):
    questions = [
        "What fact does this alert prove, and what does it only suggest?",
        "Which evidence item supports the claim?",
        "What normal-business explanation must be ruled out?"
    ]
    if asset.get("criticality") in ("high", "critical"):
        questions.append("What business service or recovery objective could be affected?")
    if alert.get("requires_privacy_review"):
        questions.append("Should privacy/legal/compliance be consulted before external communication?")
    if any(x in ("Collection", "Exfiltration") for x in alert.get("attack_mapping", [])):
        questions.append("What data-access evidence is needed before claiming data exposure?")
    return questions


def build_timeline(alerts, evidence):
    events = []
    for alert in alerts:
        events.append({
            "time": alert["timestamp_utc"],
            "kind": "alert",
            "id": alert["id"],
            "source": alert["source"],
            "description": alert["summary"]
        })
    for item in evidence:
        events.append({
            "time": item["collected_at_utc"],
            "kind": "evidence_collected",
            "id": item["id"],
            "source": item["source"],
            "description": f"Evidence collected by {item['collector']}"
        })
    return sorted(events, key=lambda event: parse_time(event["time"]))


def declare_incident(triage_records):
    p1 = [r for r in triage_records if r["priority"] == "priority-1"]
    p2 = [r for r in triage_records if r["priority"] == "priority-2"]
    privacy = any(r["privacy_review"] for r in triage_records)
    if p1 or len(p2) >= 2:
        status = "declare incident for capstone analysis"
    else:
        status = "continue investigation"
    return {
        "status": status,
        "rationale": "Declaration is based on correlated identity, endpoint, cloud, network, helpdesk, and backup evidence in the fictional dataset.",
        "privacy_legal_escalation": privacy,
        "highest_priority_alerts": [r["alert_id"] for r in triage_records if r["priority"] in ("priority-1", "priority-2")]
    }


def recommendations(declaration):
    actions = [
        "Preserve referenced evidence exports and document chain of custody before destructive containment.",
        "Ask the authorized identity owner to review the suspected account, revoke active sessions if approved, and validate MFA state.",
        "Ask the application owner to review portal administration activity and confirm whether the export job was authorized.",
        "Ask cloud/security owners to retain audit logs beyond normal retention if the case remains active.",
        "Confirm recovery readiness using backup job evidence before recommending rebuild or restore actions.",
        "Prepare a fact/inference/unknown incident note for leadership, privacy, legal, and operations."
    ]
    if declaration.get("privacy_legal_escalation"):
        actions.append("Do not make data-exposure or notification claims until privacy/legal review evaluates supported facts.")
    return actions


def main():
    data = load_package()
    warnings = []
    warnings.extend(check_scope(data))
    warnings.extend(evidence_warnings(data.get("evidence_items", [])))
    assets = index_by_id(data.get("assets", []))
    evidence_index = index_by_id(data.get("evidence_items", []))
    triage, triage_warnings = build_triage(data.get("alerts", []), assets, evidence_index)
    warnings.extend(triage_warnings)
    timeline = build_timeline(data.get("alerts", []), data.get("evidence_items", []))
    declaration = declare_incident(triage)
    output = {
        "organization": data.get("organization"),
        "drill_id": data.get("drill_id"),
        "warnings": warnings,
        "triage_records": triage,
        "timeline": timeline,
        "incident_decision": declaration,
        "recommended_next_actions": recommendations(declaration),
        "response_constraints": data.get("response_constraints", [])
    }
    OUTPUT.write_text(json.dumps(output, indent=2), encoding="utf-8")
    print("Detection, IR, and forensic evidence drill")
    print("Organization:", output["organization"])
    print("Drill:", output["drill_id"])
    print("Alerts triaged:", len(triage))
    print("Timeline events:", len(timeline))
    print("Decision:", declaration["status"])
    print("Highest priority alerts:", ", ".join(declaration["highest_priority_alerts"]))
    print()
    print("Top triage records:")

        print(f"- {record['alert_id']} | {record['priority']} | score={record['score']} | {record['asset']} | {record['summary']}")
    print()

        print("Warnings to resolve before final submission:")
        for warning in warnings:
