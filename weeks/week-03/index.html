Week 3

Cryptography

Week 3 explains how cryptography helps systems protect confidentiality, integrity, authenticity, and key establishment. The week begins from ordinary communication problems and gradually moves toward modern cryptographic engineering, key management, TLS, and post-quantum migration awareness.

Estimated reading time12–18 hours
Difficulty levelBeginner → MS foundation
Required backgroundWeeks 0–2 recommended
Major outputCryptography misuse and key-handling lab report

Overview

This week does not ask students to invent cryptographic algorithms. It teaches how to understand cryptographic goals, use standard algorithms correctly, recognize dangerous misuse, and communicate clearly with engineers, auditors, and security teams about cryptographic risk.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain why cryptography exists and which security goals it can and cannot provide.
  • Distinguish encoding, hashing, encryption, message authentication, digital signatures, and key exchange.
  • Explain why randomness, key length, nonce uniqueness, and key management matter.
  • Use safe local examples to calculate hashes, verify HMACs, generate random tokens, and inspect TLS certificates.
  • Recognize common cryptographic failures such as hardcoded keys, weak randomness, unauthenticated encryption, certificate validation bypasses, and password-hashing mistakes.
  • Describe current research directions including post-quantum cryptography, crypto agility, side-channel resistance, and formal verification.

Required Background

No cryptography background is required. Students should understand files, command-line basics, Python basics, networking basics, identity, and access-control concepts from Weeks 0–2. The lesson explains the required math before using it.

Lessons

  1. Lesson 3.1: Cryptography from First Principles ready
  2. Lesson 3.2: Randomness, Keys, Hashes, and MACs ready
  3. Lesson 3.3: Symmetric Encryption and Authenticated Encryption ready
  4. Lesson 3.4: Public-Key Cryptography, Certificates, and TLS ready
  5. Lesson 3.5: Crypto Misuse, Key Management, and Crypto Agility ready

Week 3 Output

By the end of Week 3, students will produce a safe cryptography analysis report that distinguishes correct cryptographic goals from misuse, documents local experiments, and recommends safe key-handling practices for a fictional application.
